Facebook has been a growing garbage pile in my eyes for many years. It used to feel comfy, small, and cool for a middleschooler. Pokes, interesting pictures from Canon Rebels, and the occasional funny gif compromised my feed as a young lad.

Those days have passed. 

Last week I took a look at what value it brought me. I came to the conclusion that it wasn’t much. The only thing I see in the main feed are now:
* Political topics
* Paragraphs of sad family issues/ drama you shouldn’t be posting on the internet
* Unfunny memes
* Ads, ads, ads (95% which weren’t relevant to me)

I could go on, but as a social media platform it’s become stale and thin, sort of stretched like butter scraped over too much bread.

It accumulated to a depressing experience, one of which I will not be returning to.

Twitter and Instagram feels at least fun, light, and playful (depending on who or what you follow).

I feel happy knowing that source of displeasure is gone from my life.

My advice if you’re feeling depressed from Facebook:

1. Delete Facebook. There are plenty of other social media platforms where you can get your fix of dopamine. And if anyone asks, “what about extended family that wants to keep up with you?” Give them your website, email, and/or phone number. If they love you, they’ll find a way to keep in touch.
2. Bookmark gusguidry.com under your favorites and subscribe to my RSS feed to get updates.
3. Follow @gusguidry on Instagram and Twitter.

Peace.

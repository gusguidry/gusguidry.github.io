When you receive obvious copy and pasted messages from recruiters and you are looking for a new role, **do not ignore them**. Instead, respond with your own copy and pasted message:

> Hi [spammer name],
> 
> Thank you for reaching out! I am currently interested in learning about new opportunities. However, I am receiving a lot of messages from recruiters and companies at the moment, and I am unable to follow up with everyone. Before I decide to set up a call, would you send me additional details about the role? I am interested in:
> * The company name,
> * What technology the role would be working with,
> * What kinds of problems the role will be working on, 
> * The role's location or if it's remote, 
> * What compensation range the company has in mind, 
> * and what their interview process is.

> Thank you again for reaching out!

> Sincerely,

> [your name]

Tweak the list of details in the middle (e.g., delete the technology question if the recruiter's initial message had that info) before sending. Ignore them if they respond without answering any questions. Otherwise, you will have to decide if you wish to engage based on the answers they provide.

This is not my own advice. I read this from a post on 4chan's /g/ technology board. Nonetheless, it is helpful in case anyone is looking for new opportunities.
